General Rules of Conduct

Basic Rules to follow in the lab:

  • Never work in the lab without instructor supervision.
  • No unauthorized experiments — follow the given procedure exactly.
  • No eating, drinking or chewing gum in the lab.
  • Keep your bench tidy; clean shared areas after use.
  • Report spills, broken glass, or injuries immediately.
  • Mobiles are prohibited — they can ignite volatile liquids.
  • No running or disruptive behaviour. Be alert.

Dress Code & Personal Safety

Protect yourself — wear appropriate clothes and PPE.

  • Wear a cotton lab apron and safety goggles.
  • Cover your feet — no sandals or chappals during lab sessions.
  • No long loose sleeves or dangling jewelry — tie long hair back.
  • Contact lenses are discouraged — vapours can be trapped beneath them.

Handling Chemicals

Treat all chemicals as hazardous unless instructed otherwise.

  • Do not touch, eat, or smell chemicals unless instructed. Use the wafting technique to smell safely.
  • Add acid to water when diluting — ADD ACID (never the reverse).
  • Never pipette by mouth. Keep flammables away from open flames.
  • Never return unused chemicals to stock bottles to avoid contamination.

Handling Spills & Accidents

What to do immediately after a spill or exposure.

  • Notify the instructor immediately for chemical spills.
  • Rinse affected skin or eyes with large amounts of water for at least 15 minutes.
  • Mercury spills must be reported — do not attempt to clean up yourself.
  • For fires: STOP, DROP, ROLL if clothing catches fire; use fire blanket when available.

Glassware & Bunsen Burners

  • Do not use cracked or chipped glassware.
  • Stand back while lighting a Bunsen burner and never leave it unattended.
  • Check gas hoses for cracks before use; turn off gas outlets when finished.
